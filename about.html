My name is Renata.

I was diagnosed with Breast Cancer in March 2022.

I tried telephone support, online support groups, and chat support groups, but nothing worked for me. I prefer to be in the same room with the people I am talking to. I find in-person groups can be less distracting, and offer an excuse to get out of our houses.

Unfortunately, due to the Covid-19 restrictions, in-person groups are still not an option, at the end of 2024.

When I participated in the "LiveStrong® at the YMCA" program, together with my mates, we started to meet every month for lunch.
Our lunches became something to look forward to, because they were a great opportunity to talk with someone who could really understand what we are going through.

With time, the group started growing, involving the people participating in the following sessions of the "LiveStrong® at the YMCA" program.

Also, being myself an employee of the Kenosha YMCA, I got in touch with other people dealing with cancer's side effects. By talking with them, I realized that I am able to offer them support, comfort, and empathy. That’s when I had the idea to create an in-person support group for people who have or have had cancer and that can benefit from the group.

I understand that Covid-19 scared us, but, after five years, it is now time to put patients' needs first.
Nobody wants to be a member of the cancer patients community; however, as we have been forced into this community, we better help each other.

I started thinking about what I can and cannot offer, then I found a name to identify this particular group.

The name Waves of Resilience represents the perseverance required on this special journey.
The waves represent the ups and downs of our mood and our body strength; resilience represent the new life of a cancer patient.

After I noted down ideas about what I can and cannot offer, and then the name, I was looking for a place that was quiet, private, and also free, because I don't want to ask for any money. A friend suggested the library and the Southwest Neighborhood Library in Kenosha is the one that offers several rooms with different capacity. I went to talk with someone there to see if that was an option and I reserved the room.

At this point, the only thing missing was a logo. For this, as well as for the name, I worked with my husband.
I wanted the waves, of course, but also a sun representing a new day, a new life rising after the storm. As a final touch we added a hand offering a little plant, like a hand offering help, support, and empathy.

And here we are, with this project that I honestly hope will be a success.
